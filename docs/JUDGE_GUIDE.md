# Judge Guide — Risk Sovereignty

## The 90-second path

1. Start with the Manufacturing example and set the seven **Bad weather** sliders to zero. The result is **Signal**, 36.0+ months, with Concentration as the first weak point.
2. Increase revenue decline, margin compression, fixed cost, payment delay, customer loss, called debt, and inventory impairment. At a moderate case the same balance sheet becomes **Trend**, runway falls to 9.3 months, and Collection becomes the first failure.
3. Push the stress farther. The result becomes **Emergency**, runway reaches 0.0 months, the immediate liquidity shock exceeds cash, and Cash becomes the first failure.
4. Open **History Calibration**. Enter any two or more months; gaps are allowed. The engine converts observed adverse movement into a visible Guarded, Pessimistic, or Extreme anchor. Profile-specific floors and sensitivity are disclosed, while peer data never overwrites the user's facts.
5. Switch among **Employer business**, **Independent / sole proprietor**, and **Individual / household**. Each structure keeps its own accounting meaning and its own stress ranges.
6. In household mode, inspect labor income, asset income, carrying costs, income interruption, accessible reserves, and asset-value loss. Liquidity loss stays separate from noncash repricing.
7. Open **GPT-5.6 AI red team** and enter:

   > Revenue is falling and our largest customer pays late. What should I unwind first to survive without trapping the business?

8. Generate the report. The model must use the deterministic engine first. Inspect the verified result, causal chain, **Sovereignty gate**, and exactly three actions: stop bleeding, preserve a partial exit, rebuild optionality.
9. End at **Auditable boundary**. Confirm that user input, stress assumptions, formulas, and AI judgment remain visibly separate, and that the audit strip exposes the live GPT-5.6 workflow and response IDs.

## What GPT-5.6 does

The server forces GPT-5.6 to call `calculate_stress_test`. The model does not own the financial results. The submitted case is normalized and calculated by the local deterministic engine. A second GPT-5.6 pass receives that verified result and must return a strict JSON Schema report. A local semantic audit checks the sovereignty gate, action order, and every evidence reference before the report reaches the UI.

The live judge-demo result correctly avoids the all-or-nothing answer. It recommends freezing new customer-specific exposure first rather than closing the whole business; negotiating debt and releasing only surplus inventory in tranches; and then rebuilding cash reserves, deposit requirements, billing milestones, and exposure caps.

## Why this is not a wrapper

| Layer | Owner | Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Subject facts | User | AI cannot silently rewrite them |
| Stress assumptions | User | Visible and editable |
| Historical anchor | Deterministic calibration | Uses supplied months, disclosed multipliers, and visible profile floors |
| Financial outputs | Deterministic engine | Formula-based, testable, and traceable |
| Interpretation | GPT-5.6 | Challenges assumptions and designs staged exits |
| Method audit | Local validator | Rejects invented evidence and broken action order |

The product is cash-flow decision support, not a GAAP statement or accounting, tax, legal, lending, credit, bankruptcy, benefits, or investment advice.

## Core idea

Risk Sovereignty does not promise to predict the future. It simulates a bad one, finds the first binding constraint, and helps the user prevent that future from removing every next move. Once downside is bounded and a real exit exists, the user can participate in upside more courageously—not less.
