# Demo Script — 2:45 Maximum

Language: English audio with English captions. Optional Chinese captions may appear below the English line.

## 0:00–0:15 — Hook

**Screen:** Start on the runway diagnosis with the loaded manufacturing case.

**Voice:**

> Most business AI tells an owner how to grow. Risk Sovereignty asks a harder question: when the world stops cooperating, what breaks first—and which move preserves the right to choose again tomorrow?

## 0:15–0:35 — The diagnosis

**Screen:** Point to zero runway, the failed six-month test, and the weakest lifeline.

**Voice:**

> This company appears profitable before stress. But delayed collections, a partial debt call, and inventory impairment consume more cash than it holds. The first failure is cash—not because it is the most dramatic risk, but because it is the weakest constraint.

## 0:35–1:05 — Deterministic truth

**Screen:** Scroll to Business facts and Bad weather. Move revenue drop and payment delay.

**Voice:**

> The numbers are not generated by AI. Business facts and stress assumptions stay visible and editable. A deterministic engine recalculates stressed revenue, monthly cash flow, one-time shock, runway, and five lifelines in real time. Industry presets are only editable demo data.

## 1:05–1:30 — Staged survival

**Screen:** Open the four survival stages and highlight the active stage.

**Voice:**

> Risk Sovereignty avoids the false choice between doing nothing and shutting down. It removes exposure in stages, verifies that the claimed exit actually exists, and preserves resources to participate again. This is not hiding from risk; bounded downside is what makes a bold next move possible.

## 1:30–2:15 — GPT-5.6 red team

**Screen:** Enter: “Our largest customer wants 30 more payment days. Equipment needs 45 days to sell.” Run the report. Show loading, causal chain, and three actions.

**Voice:**

> GPT-5.6 is not allowed to invent financial truth. The server forces a strict function call to the stress-test engine. A second pass must complete the sovereignty gate—exit reality, tolerable loss, re-entry, preserved upside, and decision quality—before producing exactly three staged actions.

## 2:15–2:35 — Evidence and audit

**Screen:** Show evidence IDs and the Input / Assumption / Calculation / AI cards.

**Voice:**

> Every action cites calculation or assumption evidence. A local method audit rejects imaginary evidence or broken phase order. The interface keeps user facts, editable assumptions, deterministic calculations, and AI judgment visibly separate.

> The final audit strip exposes the model name, the forced tool workflow, and both OpenAI response IDs as proof of a live run.

## 2:35–2:45 — Close

**Screen:** Return to the hero and switch briefly to Chinese, then back to English.

**Voice:**

> Risk Sovereignty does not predict the future. It controls exposure, preserves exit rights, and helps a small-business owner keep the next move.

## Recording checklist

- Keep the final cut below 3:00; target 2:35–2:50.
- Record at 1920×1080, 100% browser zoom, with the cursor visible.
- Use real GPT-5.6 output in the final take; do not record the local fallback notice.
- Add English captions and ensure all UI text remains legible after video compression.
- Do not show API keys, account emails, browser bookmarks, or unrelated tabs.
- End on the product, not on Devpost or the code editor.
